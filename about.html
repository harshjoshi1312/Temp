2.1 - US Institute of 3D Printing Technology (USI3DT)

The US Institute of 3D Technology (USI3DT) California, USA, inspired by President Obama’s initiative and “America Makes,” was established to cater to the upcoming demand for professionals in 3D Printing Technology.

The objective of USI3DT is to impart education and training to students and professionals from various industries to maintain pace with future technology.

2.2 3D Systems Corporation

3D Systems, headquartered in Rock Hill, South Carolina, USA, is a company that engineers and manufactures 3D Printers. Mr. Chuck Hull invented 3D Printing Technology (Stereo-lithography) in 1986 and founded 3D Systems Corporation. 3D Systems is a pioneer in the 3D Printing domain with a fleet of technologies like SLA, SLS, DLP, 3D Scanning & 3D Designing & Inspection, and metal 3D Printing Technology. The Company has more than 1,000 U.S. and foreign patents. It has customers in 80 countries, is headquartered in Rock Hill, South Carolina, and has over 25 worldwide locations. More than 37 years ago, 3D Systems brought the innovation of 3D Printing to the manufacturing industry. The leading Additive Manufacturing solutions company empowers manufacturers to create products and business models never before possible through transformed workflows. It has achieved the Company’s best-of-breed digital manufacturing ecosystem comprising plastic and metal 3D Printers, print materials, on-demand manufacturing services, and a portfolio of end-to-end manufacturing software. Each solution is powered by the expertise of the Company’s application engineers, who collaborate with customers to transform manufacturing environments. 3D Systems’ solutions address various advanced applications for prototyping through production in markets such as aerospace, automotive, medical, dental, and consumer goods.
